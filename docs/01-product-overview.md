# Product Overview

Metopus is an early-stage artist and fan platform designed around direct artist/fan connection rather than dependence on algorithmic social platforms.

The broad idea is simple: artists need a place they can own, shape and maintain without losing the relationship to third-party feeds. Fans need a clearer way to follow artists, receive updates, join communities, access exclusive content and move between public discovery and deeper app-based experiences.

## Product Goals

- Give artists a public web presence that can be shared, indexed and linked from QR codes, posters, social profiles and live shows.
- Let artists capture mailing-list interest without forcing an account signup.
- Create richer fan experiences inside native app surfaces where performance, interaction and push notification behaviour matter.
- Support artist-controlled community, updates, media, releases, events and fan engagement.
- Use shared logic where appropriate so web, Android and iOS do not drift into separate products.

## Target Users

- Independent artists and bands who need direct audience ownership.
- Fans who want artist updates without relying entirely on social feeds.
- Promoters, venues and press contacts who need quick public information.
- Artists who need practical tools for campaigns, events, community and fan communication.

## Current Public-Safe Scope

Public discussion can safely include:

- artist profile and fan-capture concepts
- public marketing and website strategy
- cross-platform app architecture
- shared Rust logic at a high level
- responsive interface and visual-system work
- testing and device coverage
- AI-assisted development workflow

Public discussion should avoid:

- exact private database schemas
- service-role or admin implementation details
- proprietary reward, ranking or monetisation mechanics
- unpublished commercial plans
- private source code
- unreleased assets or third-party copyrighted material

## Product Positioning

Metopus sits at the intersection of:

- direct-to-fan music technology
- mobile-first community software
- public artist discovery
- digital product design
- interactive visual systems
- creator-owned audience infrastructure

The case study should not try to sell every future feature. Its job is to show the level of product and implementation thinking behind the work.
